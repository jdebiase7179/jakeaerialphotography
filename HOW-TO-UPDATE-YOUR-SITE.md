# How to update your site (for Jake)

Your site: **https://jdebiase7179.github.io/jakeaerialphotography/**

You don't need to install anything or write any code, ever. Everything
below happens on the GitHub website in a normal browser, logged in as you.
Do it from a computer — the phone app doesn't do uploads well.

---

## Add photos or videos

1. Open **https://github.com/jdebiase7179/jakeaerialphotography/tree/main/photos**
   (bookmark this)
2. Click the folder the shots belong in:
   - `aerial` — general drone shots
   - `real-estate` — listings
   - `events` — events
   - `automotive` — car stuff
   - `trap-houses` — the abandoned houses section
3. Click **Add file** (top right) → **Upload files**
4. Drag your photos/videos into the box
5. Click the green **Commit changes** button

**Wait about 2 minutes, refresh your site, they're live.** Big files
straight off the drone are fine — the site shrinks them automatically.
iPhone photos (HEIC) are fine too.

Rules:
- Videos: keep each clip under **90 MB** (export a short cut, not the
  whole flight)
- Name files what you want the caption to say: `sunset-over-the-marina.jpg`
  shows as "Sunset over the marina". Names like `DJI_0231.jpg` work too,
  they just get a blank-ish caption.

## Remove a photo

1. Same photos page as above, click into the folder, click the file
2. Click the **trash can** icon (top right of the file view)
3. Green **Commit changes** button

Gone from the site in ~2 minutes.

## Change words on the site (email, prices, your bio)

Easiest: text Ben. It takes him one sentence to his Claude.

If you want to try yourself: open `index.html` on GitHub, click the
**pencil** icon, carefully change only the words between the `>` and `<`
marks, then **Commit changes**. If the site looks broken afterward, tell
Ben — nothing is ever lost, everything can be undone.

## If something looks wrong

Nothing you do through these steps can permanently break anything —
every change is saved and reversible. Just tell Ben what you did.
